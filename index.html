Morphological data remains an integral part of species identification, even with the advent of techniques in molecular biology. It is particularly essential for identification of fossil species, and also with unidentified specimens that remain in zoology and natural history museum collections. Here, a previously unidentified specimen from the Grant Museum of Zoology is described through observations of key morphological and anatomical structures and identified using comparative anatomy from the Grant Museum collection and relevant scientific literature. After identification, the wider literature is used to review the phylogeny at genus and family level, biogeography of the species and the evolutionary implications. Finally, conservation issues for the specimen and threats to their habitat will be explored.
